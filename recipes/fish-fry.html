Fabulous Fish Fry

Description

This is List's perfect fish fry recipe. If you don't like fish, then what exactly are you doing here? To be persuaded?

If that's the case, then look no further than here. Heck, even I'm not that big a fan of seafood! Yet whenever anyone tries this, they say it's fuego.

Ingredients

  • White fish - any will do, but my personal favorites are cod and halibut
  • Panko - Japanese electrocuted bread crumbs!
  • Eggs
  • Flour, as much as one needs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Old Bay
  • Paprika
  • Lemon - for freshness
  • Oil - preferably a canola, rapeseed, or vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Coat your fish in flour. Make sure you coat every surface, including all the cracks and crevices.
  2. Dip your floured fish into an egg wash until it's all wet.
  3. Coat your wetted floured fish in panko. Like the flour, get all the fish's nooks and crannies.
  4. Let your fish sit for a few minutes. This will give you enough time to heat your pan at medium, filled halfway with oil.
  5. Set your fish for 10-15 minutes on each side until golden brown. Be sure to cook it slowly, lest you deal with cleaning your stovetop and eating burnt fish later.
  6. After you're done cooking, set your fish on a wire rack or a plate with a paper towel to absorb excess fat, then let it cool down for a few more minutes.
  7. Squeeze some lemon on those bad boys and enjoy!
